Vertical CRM · UX Research · Complex Domain
Care Coordination Platform — 0-to-1 Vertical CRM
A care organisation delivering personalised support for individuals with complex neurological conditions, brain injuries, and physical or learning disabilities had a fundamental product problem: care coordination — matching community workers to clients, scheduling social activities, logging observations, and billing across local authority and NHS funding streams — was being managed without software built for the job. The stakes were not abstract: wrong matches, missed risk notes, or billing errors directly affected vulnerable people and the workers caring for them.

Kevin and his UX researchers spent weeks onsite with three distinct user groups — organisation staff, community workers (those supporting clients in social settings such as day trips, shopping, and outings), and client guardians (legal guardians or responsible parties). The team ran structured discovery sessions, then moved into user persona mapping, user journey mapping, value proposition definition, and metrics before a single prototype was drawn.

The platform Kevin and his team designed and built covered five interconnected use cases:

• Client onboarding — needs assessment, care plan documentation, billing setup, and guardian relationship management
• Community worker onboarding — skills profiling, availability, certification tracking (DBS, specialist training), and compliance requirements
• Intelligent matching — client paired to worker against skills, availability, and care plan; private clinical notes released to the matched worker only at point of assignment — privacy-preserving, role-gated disclosure
• Social activity log — post-visit write-ups: observations, risks, incidents; reviewed by staff who issued guidance and set safety policies for future visits; an auditable, continuously improving care record
• Billing and invoicing — flexible multi-payer billing to individuals, businesses, and local authorities funding care through UK benefits and NHS — each with distinct invoicing formats, payment terms, and compliance requirements

Kevin was personally involved across the full lifecycle — discovery through CSG — serving as executive sponsor throughout. The matching and private-notes architecture was the hardest design challenge: balancing care plan transparency with client privacy, worker safety obligations, and GDPR data minimisation simultaneously.

The Fractional CPO — Embedded Product Leadership
Kevin joined Salesforce London as an individual contributor — no team, no direct reports. What he built was something harder to see on an org chart: a product methodology that transformed how an entire function operated, through personal craft and influence alone.

It was during this chapter that Kevin first read Dan Olsen's The Lean Product Playbook (2015) — and it became his operating framework. The Product-Market Fit Pyramid gave Kevin a structured, repeatable way to diagnose where a product was failing and what to fix first: start with the target customer, understand their underserved needs, define the value proposition, build the feature set, and only then focus on UX. Applied to ISV partner engagements, it replaced assumption-driven product conversations with evidence-backed ones.

Kevin rebuilt every partner engagement around this foundation, adding three complementary practices:

• Design Sprints — 5-day structured cycles from problem to tested prototype; partners left with validated product concepts, not vague requirements or slide decks
• Lean Startup — build-measure-learn loops replacing assumption-driven roadmaps with evidence-backed priorities
• Business Model Canvas — a shared commercial language forcing alignment on value proposition, revenue model, and customer segments before engineering began

Kevin operated as a Fractional CPO inside each partner organisation — running discovery, mapping user personas and journeys, defining value propositions, co-building roadmaps, and setting product strategy alongside founders, PMs, and engineering teams. Kevin had no authority over any of them. The outcomes — double-digit ACV growth year-on-year across EMEA — reflect what happens when product rigour is applied as a discipline, not a service.
