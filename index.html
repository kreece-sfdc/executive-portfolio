Commercial Innovation · Deal Structuring · 0-to-1
PAELA · $100M ACV Pipeline
PAELA — the Partner Agentic Enterprise License Agreement — is a commercial vehicle I co-developed from first principles. Nothing like it existed before at Salesforce.

The core insight was that the most strategic ISV partners have a dual relationship with Salesforce: they are simultaneously a customer (consuming Salesforce licenses and platform capacity to build and run their own products) and a partner (reselling Salesforce-embedded applications to their own OEM customers). Every existing commercial structure treated these as separate relationships — separate contracts, separate teams, separate negotiations. PAELA conflates them into a single, unified agreement for the first time.

A PAELA deal covers:

• Direct licenses and consumption — the ISV's own Salesforce licenses and platform consumption credits for internal use, building and running their product
• OEM customer licenses — Salesforce licenses bundled alongside the ISV's own app licenses, enabling the ISV to sell a complete Salesforce-powered solution to their end customers
• Consumption credits for OEM customers — extending platform consumption entitlements through the ISV's customer base, aligning Salesforce's revenue model to actual usage at scale

Commercially, PAELA aligns to the EDA (Enterprise Distribution Agreement) framework for OEMs — but adds consumption economics for the first time, directly tying Salesforce's revenue to how deeply the ISV's customers use the platform. It is a One Salesforce deal: one agreement, one commercial relationship, one aligned incentive structure across what were previously two separate motions.

With 5 deals in flight targeting $100M ACV in FY27, PAELA is one of the most consequential commercial structures I have built in my career.
